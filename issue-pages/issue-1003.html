So far, square brackets have been used to indicate corrections made by the editorial team. Corrections are conveyed in a straightforward manner, supplying either missing letters, striking through extra ones, or some similar direct correction in the TCN. E.g., "il[z]" There is also talk of rendering the content of `<sup>` tags within square brackets (rather than in bold, as they are now), with the word "supplied." E.g., "oil of [supplied: sulfur]" Nick has recently implemented a rendering solution for gap tags (`<gap/>`) and illegible tags (`<ill/>`). For the former, the word "gap" is given in square brackets: [gap]. For the latter, the word "illegible" is given in square brackets: [illegible]. I am concerned that it might look as though we are supplying text. One way to differentiate would be to italicize the words "gap" and "illegible." Thoughts?
