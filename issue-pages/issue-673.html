673: Massicot — keep in Fr?

**Glossary:** Massicot (m.) (U) = keep Fr. “massicot”, i.e. lead-tin yellow See MinDat. DMF: chaux de plomb de couleur jaune ; Cotgrave: oaker (i.e. ochre) made of ceruse or white lead. See Kirby et al., Pigment Glossary: (Note that the word ‘massicot’ today refers to a pale yellow lead monoxide; in earlier times it referred to lead-tin oxide.) There are 15 instances of "massicot," but only 2 have been kept in Fr in translation campaigns. Is it not enough to translate it and make the note about its historical composition in the glossary?
