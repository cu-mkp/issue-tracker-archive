1883: 166r_1: translation of "inventeurs"

From #1469 The translation for the following sentence changes "inventeurs" from a noun to a verb (and in the process loses a `<pro>` tag). One option would be to add `<pro>` to "invented" but this does not make for as close a translation and creates inconsistency for other "invent"-as-a-verb occurrences within this same entry (not to mention the ms at large). https://edition640.makingandknowing.org/#/folios/166r/tl/166r/tcn TCN: > Pythagore* & aultres de Tyanée ont aprins par leurs peregrinations les disciplines desquels ilz se sont dicts inventeurs en leur pays, &[c]. TL: > Pythagoras* & others from Tyana learned by their travels the disciplines which they claimed to have invented in their country, &c. What do you recommend, @ps2270
